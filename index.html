What

Infrastructure, not a product

Attestto Open assembles open standards into deployable infrastructure for verifiable digital systems. Open schemas, decentralized identifiers, and cryptographic verification that any government, institution, or developer can adopt and extend — with no single-vendor lock-in.

◇

Open schemas

Machine-readable data models that define how verifiable information is structured. Domain-agnostic, extensible by design.

⟁

Decentralized trust

No central authority controls the system. Trust is cryptographic, distributed across institutions and private companies alike, and verifiable by anyone.

⊘

Privacy at every layer

Selective disclosure, zero-knowledge proofs, and minimal data exposure — integrated at the architectural level, not just the protocol layer, so the privacy guarantees hold end-to-end.

Three-layer verification

Every piece of verifiable data can be checked at three independent layers. Any two of three is sufficient for full verification.

The system degrades gracefully, never catastrophically.

01 Institutional Issuer resolves via DID document hosted at their domain
02 Registry Schema and status hosted on a neutral registry (CDN, IPFS)
03 Ledger Cryptographic proof anchored on a public, immutable ledger
Principles

What we stand for

These principles are non-negotiable. Every component we build, every standard we propose, and every governance decision we make is measured against them.

Sovereignty

You control your own data and infrastructure. No external dependency required to operate.

Interoperability

Built on W3C, IETF, and ISO standards. If your system is compliant, it works with ours — and vice versa.

Privacy by design

Data minimization at every layer. Verifiers learn only what they need. Consent is cryptographically enforced, not policy-based.

No vendor lock-in

Every component is open source, every format is open standard. You can fork it, replace it, or run it yourself. Forever.

Reversibility

Any deployment can export its data in open formats and migrate to another implementation. No trapped data, ever.

Transparency

Governance decisions, technical choices, and roadmap priorities are public. You can see exactly how and why things change.

Technological neutrality

Blockchain, PKI, cloud, on-premise — the architecture supports all. You pick the stack, not us.

Public infrastructure

The open layer is infrastructure for everyone. Attestto builds products on top — but the foundation is free to use and extend.

Governance

Stewardship, not control

Attestto Open is stewarded, not owned. The governance model is designed so that no single entity — including Attestto — can unilaterally control the infrastructure, the standards, or the roadmap.

Open governance

Technical decisions are made transparently. Proposals and discussions are public. As the community grows, so does shared decision-making.

Contributor model

Institutions, governments, and developers can propose schemas, DID methods, and protocol extensions through a structured contribution process.

No single owner

The infrastructure is designed to outlive any single organization. Forkable, portable, and built for community governance from day one.
