What

Infrastructure, not a product

Attestto Open builds the foundational layers for verifiable digital systems. Open schemas, decentralized identifiers, and cryptographic verification that any government, institution, or developer can deploy and extend without depending on a single vendor.

◇

Open schemas

Machine-readable data models that define how verifiable information is structured. Domain-agnostic, extensible by design.

⟁

Decentralized trust

No central authority controls the system. Trust is cryptographic, distributed across institutions, and verifiable by anyone.

⊘

Privacy by construction

Selective disclosure, zero-knowledge proofs, and minimal data exposure are built into the protocol, not bolted on.

Principles

What we stand for

These principles are non-negotiable. Every component we build, every standard we propose, and every governance decision we make is measured against them.

Sovereignty

Institutions and individuals control their own data and infrastructure. No external dependency required to operate.

Interoperability

Built on W3C, IETF, and ISO standards. Systems built on Attestto Open interoperate with any compliant implementation.

Privacy by design

Data minimization at every layer. Verifiers learn only what they need, nothing more. Consent is cryptographically enforced.

No vendor lock-in

Every component is open source, every format is open standard. You can fork it, replace it, or run it yourself. Forever.

Reversibility

Any deployment can export its data in open formats and migrate to another implementation. No trapped data, ever.

Transparency

Governance decisions, technical choices, and roadmap priorities are public. Stewardship, not control.

Technological neutrality

The infrastructure does not prescribe specific technologies. Blockchain, PKI, cloud, on-premise — the architecture supports all.

Public good

This is infrastructure for society, not a product for sale. Built to be adopted, not purchased.

Architecture

Three-layer verification

Every piece of verifiable data can be checked at three independent layers. Any two of three is sufficient. No single point of failure.

01 Institutional Issuer resolves via DID document hosted at their domain
02 Registry Schema and status hosted on a neutral registry (CDN, IPFS)
03 Ledger Cryptographic proof anchored on a public, immutable ledger

Any two of three layers is sufficient to verify. The system degrades gracefully, never catastrophically.

Governance

Stewardship, not control

Attestto Open is stewarded, not owned. The governance model ensures that no single entity — including Attestto — can unilaterally control the infrastructure, the standards, or the roadmap.

Open governance

Technical decisions are made transparently. Proposals, discussions, and votes are public. Anyone can participate.

Contributor model

Institutions, governments, and developers can propose schemas, DID methods, and protocol extensions through a structured contribution process.

No single owner

The infrastructure is designed to outlive any single organization. Forkable, portable, and governed by its community.
